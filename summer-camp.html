Summer Camp

Camp Don Lee’s summer program is built around a group model that includes eight girls and eight boys of approximately the same age led by two trained and experienced counselors. The group is also supported by two program staff members from the waterfront, pool, craft, nature or operations staff. Small group camping is the unique trademark of the Don Lee experience. Within this model, the group grows in trust and faith as they work together, play together, and worship together. Year after year campers and alumni tell us that “the group” was so essential to the joy and excitement of their camp experience. Don Lee focuses on building the community of the group. Activities are fun and exciting but “the group” is where the real experience is built. Groups do not always start out in perfect relationships, but with willingness and sharing, growth in trust and trustworthiness can lift the experience to a real high moment in a camper’s life.

Early Camp

Looking for one week camps at Don Lee? We are excited to offer opportunities for elementary and youth one-week campers to come to camp before summer officially begins, during our Early Camp Weeks. Campers will have an opportunity to participate in every activity at camp including sailing, archery, swimming, arts and crafts, nature, and more! All sessions are one week traditional.

Two Week Camps

Whether campers are coming with friends or on their own, they’ll become part of a small group family that is truly special to Don Lee. Campers participate in camp activities and learn new skills like archery, canoeing, kayaking, sailing, outdoor living skills, and more. They also will form a community through team building and nightly vespers. Swimming in the pool, campfires, field games, dances, talent show and more will be the highlight of the two week experience. Each two week group will lead a worship.

Mariners

Make new friends while participating in multi-days sailing on the Neuse River and to Cape Lookout in Sunfish, Flying Scots, and Hobie Cats. Campers will also have the opportunity to take part in camp activities such as bible studies, arts and crafts, swimming in the pool, canoeing, fishing, archery, and leading a worship service together.

Navigators

This three-week high adventure camp will challenge experienced sailors with an exciting trip across the Pamlico Sound to Portsmouth Island. The session will begin with a week of sailing drills, trip preparation and other traditional Don Lee activities. Prior sailing experience is required.

Leaders in Training

This four week leadership program is designed for older youth who are ready for a highly challenging and deeply rewarding experience. LITs will grow deeper in their faith, take part in experiences that help them learn more about themselves, and gain lifelong friendships. Certifications earned will include American Red Cross Lifeguard, American Red Cross First Aid and CPR, American Red Cross Wilderness First Aid, and U.S. Sailing Association certification as a sailing counselor. Past experience sailing or as a camper at Don Lee is not required.

About

Don Lee Camp & Retreat Center is affiliated by faith with The North Carolina Conference of the United Methodist Church and operated by NC United Methodist Camp & Retreat Ministries, Inc.

Don Lee is nestled on the north shore of the Neuse River near Oriental, North Carolina. Don Lee Camp & Retreat Center is an ideal setting to learn sailing, explore God’s creation and grow in faith.
